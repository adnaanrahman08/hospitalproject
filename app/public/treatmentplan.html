Washing/Bathing

1. Avoid using soaps/detergents to wash your skin as this can cause drying of the skin and contribute to more skin irritation. Use a soap substitute instead.
2. Wash your skin with soap substitute. Apply this in a downward direction in the direction of hair growth in a smooth downward motion when you get into your shower or bath. Leave it on your skin for a few minutes and then rinse it off.
3. Keep your washing/bathing temperature to a warm/medium heat and avoid hot showers/baths to prevent skin dehydration.
4. Showers/baths should last no longer than 10 minutes.
5. After your bathe, pat your skin dry with a towel. Avoid rubbing the skin dry as friction can aggravate your skin.
6. Then apply your moisturiser.

Topical Steroids/Anti-inflammatory treatment

1 Allow your moisturiser to soak in for 15-30 minutes before applying topical steroid/anti-inflammatory creams/ointments.
2 When you use topical steroids on the skin, use a weaker steroid on areas of the body where the skin is slightly thinner (face, neck, creases) and a stronger steroid on areas of the body where the skin is thicker (on the body).
3 Similar to your moisturiser, don't rub in cream/ointment (smooth it on and apply in the direction of hair growth).
4 Use your topical steroid/anti inflammatory cream/ointment regularly until the skin improves (typically 1-2 weeks) and then start to decrease how often you apply this treatment.
5 I suggest you do the following:
Face & Neck: {faceSteroid} one application twice daily for 1-2 weeks; then every other day for 1-2 weeks; then twice weekly for a further 1-2 weeks
Body: {bodySteroid} one application once daily 1-2 weeks; then every other day for 1-2 weeks; then twice weekly for a further 1-2 weeks
